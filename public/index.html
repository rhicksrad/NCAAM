Way-too-early awards preview

Opening week snapshots of who is setting the pace for every major trophy.

Most Valuable Player

  • Shai Gilgeous-Alexander Thunder

    35 on Opening Night with the game-winning FTs, then a 55-point career high in a 2OT Finals-rematch win. OKC opened 3-0.

  • Victor Wembanyama Spurs

    3-0 start anchored by 40 in the opener and 31-14-6 vs BKN. Western Player of the Week.

  • Giannis Antetokounmpo Bucks

    36-16-7 week on average per league’s weekly honor, plus a 40-14-9 in CLE.

Defensive Player of the Year

  • Victor Wembanyama Spurs

    6.0 blocks per, high-leverage rim deterrence in wins over DAL, NOP, BKN.

  • Evan Mobley Cavaliers

    Closed MIL with 12-for-12 at the line and anchored late stops.

  • Chet Holmgren Thunder

    Stretch-five spacing on one end, contests and glass on the other in OKC’s 3-0.

Rookie of the Year

  • VJ Edgecombe 76ers

    Historic 34 in his debut, third-most ever in an NBA debut, in a 117-116 win at BOS.

  • Cooper Flagg Mavericks

    22 with a momentum-swing dunk in DAL’s first win.

  • Dylan Harper Spurs

    20-8 off the bench to steady SA’s fourth-quarter response vs BKN.

Sixth Man of the Year

  • Ajay Mitchell Thunder

    26 off the bench in the 2OT win at IND, plus 14 vs ATL. Role impact is immediate.

  • Jaime Jaquez Jr. Heat

    17 off the bench and a +18 swing in the Heat’s home-opener win.

  • Sam Merrill Cavaliers

    17 timely points in the win over MIL.

Most Improved Player

  • Ausar Thompson Pistons

    21-12 board-crushing line in comeback over BOS, flashing a two-way leap.

  • Evan Mobley Cavaliers

    Expanded late-game usage and playmaking touches alongside the defense.

  • Cam Thomas Nets

    Volume still real, 40 at SA; efficiency and creation balance are the watch items.

Clutch Player of the Year

  • Shai Gilgeous-Alexander Thunder

    Game-deciding FTs vs HOU and a torrent of late points across two 2OT wins.

  • Tyrese Maxey 76ers

    40 in the one-point win at BOS, closing like a vet.

  • LaMelo Ball Hornets

    38-13-13 triple-double with a takeover second half.

Coach of the Year

  • Mitch Johnson Spurs

    3-0 start, clear defensive identity, and a calibrated rookie integration.

  • Mark Daigneault Thunder

    Navigated two chaotic 2OT wins then a comfortable third game.

  • J.B. Bickerstaff Pistons

    Early identity win over BOS built on size and glass.

Executive of the Year

  • Sam Presti Thunder

    Rotation depth already paying off with Ajay Mitchell’s bench pop and Isaiah Hartenstein’s minutes.

  • San Antonio front office Spurs

    Wemby’s support system plus rookie guard production fueling wins.

  • Detroit front office Pistons

    The size and glass emphasis around Cade was the difference vs BOS.
